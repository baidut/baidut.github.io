High Dynamic Range Content Generation @PKU

Supervisor: Prof. Ronggang Wang and Prof. Ge Li
Group Leader: Zhenqiang Ying
Group Member: Yurui Ren, Chao Wang

High Dynamic Range (HDR) is the latest video format for display technology and there is a strong industrial effort in deploying an HDR capable ecosystem in the near future. However, most existing video content today are in Standard Dynamic Range (SDR) format and there is a growing necessity to upscale this content for HDR displays. Tone expansion, also known as inverse tone mapping, converts an SDR content into an HDR format using Expansion Operators (EOs). In this project, we plan to adopt multi-exposure fusion framework to solve the problem. For each SDR frame, we first use CNNs to predict other exposures, then fuse those exposures to build a HDR frame.
